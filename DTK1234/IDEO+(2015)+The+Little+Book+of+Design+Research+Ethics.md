# The Little Book of Design Research Ethics

## Overview
"The Little Book of Design Research Ethics" by IDEO serves as a comprehensive guide for ethical practices in design research. It emphasizes the importance of trust, respect, and collaboration in interactions with participants. The book distills over 25 years of experience into three core principles—[[Respect]], [[Responsibility]], and [[Honesty]]—and provides practical guidelines for ethical research practices.

## Key Concepts & Definitions
- **[[Respect]]**: Honoring participants' limits and valuing their comfort.
- **[[Responsibility]]**: Acting to protect participants' current and future interests.
- **[[Honesty]]**: Maintaining truthful and timely communication with participants.
- **[[Ethics]]**: A set of moral principles that govern a person's behavior or the conducting of an activity.

## Detailed Technical Breakdown

### The Principles
1. **Respect**
   - Treat participants as collaborators, not subjects.
   - Be aware of cultural expectations and sensitivities.

2. **Responsibility**
   - Ensure research does not harm participants.
   - Obtain informed consent and safeguard sensitive information.

3. **Honesty**
   - Avoid misleading participants.
   - Clearly communicate the purpose and outcomes of research.

### The Guidelines
- **Planning and Preparation**
  - Keep the team informed about research conduct and confidentiality.
  - Seek support for ethical ambiguities from experienced colleagues.

- **Gathering Information**
  - Provide clear explanations to participants about the research.
  - Seek permission from participants rather than forgiveness.

- **Using and Sharing**
  - Maintain transparency in how information is used and shared.

| Guideline                | Description                                                                 |
|--------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Keep the team informed    | Ensure all team members understand research protocols and confidentiality.  |
| Seek support              | Consult with experienced colleagues for ethical dilemmas.                  |
| Provide clear explanations | Inform participants about the research purpose and data usage.             |
| Seek permission           | Always ask for consent before involving participants in research.          |

## Implementation/Examples
```markdown
### Example of Ethical Research Practice
In a study involving cancer treatment communication, IDEO conducted a survey with patients and caregivers. The research was designed to gather insights ethically, ensuring participants felt comfortable sharing their experiences. The findings highlighted the need for transparency regarding treatment availability and enrollment in trials.
```

> [!note] **Key Takeaway**: Ethical research practices are essential for maintaining trust and ensuring participant comfort. Always prioritize transparency and respect in interactions.
> 
> [!important] **Remember**: If you encounter ethical dilemmas, do not hesitate to seek guidance from experienced colleagues or legal counsel.
