# Designerly Ways of Knowing

## Overview
This lecture explores the theoretical foundations of design as a distinct discipline, emphasizing its role as a 'third area' of education alongside the sciences and humanities. Nigel Cross articulates the intrinsic values of design education, advocating for a shift from conventional instrumental aims to a focus on 'designerly ways of knowing.' The paper argues for the recognition of design's unique criteria and its contribution to general education.

## Key Concepts & Definitions
- **Designerly Ways of Knowing**: The unique methods and understandings specific to the design discipline.
- **Third Area of Education**: A concept introduced by Bruce Archer, referring to design as a distinct educational field separate from sciences and humanities.
- **Material Culture**: The collective experience and skills involved in the arts of planning, inventing, making, and doing.
- **Intrinsic Values**: Values that are inherent to the discipline of design, as opposed to extrinsic, vocational aims.

## Detailed Technical Breakdown

### The Nature of Design
- **Central Concern**: Conception and realization of new things.
- **Methods of Inquiry**:
  - **Design**: Modelling, pattern-formation, synthesis.
  - **Sciences**: Controlled experiments, classification, analysis.
  - **Humanities**: Analogy, metaphor, criticism, evaluation.
- **Values**:
  - **Design**: Practicality, ingenuity, empathy, appropriateness.
  - **Sciences**: Objectivity, rationality, neutrality, truth.
  - **Humanities**: Subjectivity, imagination, commitment, justice.

### Education in Design
- **Three Aspects of Education**:
  1. Transmission of knowledge.
  2. Training in methods of inquiry.
  3. Initiation into belief systems and values.
  
| Aspect                | Sciences                     | Humanities                  | Design                       |
|-----------------------|------------------------------|-----------------------------|------------------------------|
| Phenomenon of Study   | Natural world                | Human experience            | Artificial world             |
| Methods                | Controlled experiments        | Analogy, metaphor           | Modelling, synthesis         |
| Values                 | Objectivity, truth           | Subjectivity, justice       | Practicality, empathy        |

> [!important] The distinction between specialist and general education is crucial. General education must focus on intrinsic values rather than vocational training.

## Implementation/Examples
```markdown
### Example of Designerly Ways of Knowing
1. **Modelling**: Creating representations of ideas to explore possibilities.
2. **Pattern Formation**: Identifying and utilizing patterns in design processes.
3. **Synthesis**: Combining various elements to create cohesive designs.
```

> [!note] The Royal College of Art emphasizes that design education should contribute to self-realization, not just prepare students for specific careers.
