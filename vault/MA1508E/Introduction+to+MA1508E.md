# MA1508E: Linear Algebra for Engineering

## Overview
MA1508E is a foundational course in Linear Algebra tailored for engineering students, emphasizing the importance of linear systems, matrices, and vector spaces. The course integrates theoretical concepts with practical applications, preparing students for advanced topics in data analytics, machine learning, and engineering. Through a blended learning approach, students engage with content at their own pace while participating in interactive problem-solving sessions.

## Key Concepts & Definitions
- **Linear Systems**: A collection of linear equations that can be solved using various methods.
- **Matrices**: Rectangular arrays of numbers that represent linear transformations and systems of equations.
- **Euclidean Vector Spaces**: Mathematical structures that facilitate the study of vectors and their properties.
- **Eigenanalysis**: The study of eigenvalues and eigenvectors, crucial for understanding linear transformations.
- **Differential Systems**: Systems of equations involving derivatives, often analyzed using linear algebra techniques.

## Detailed Technical Breakdown

### Learning Outcomes
1. **Linear Systems**
   - Definitions and applications
   - Finding solutions

2. **Matrices and Matrix Operations**
   - Properties and operations
   - Relation to solving linear systems

3. **Euclidean Vector Spaces**
   - Properties and applications
   - Finding least square solutions to inconsistent systems

4. **Eigenanalysis and Differential Systems**
   - Algorithms and applications
