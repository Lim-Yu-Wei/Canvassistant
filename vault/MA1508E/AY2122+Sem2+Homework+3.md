# MA1508E Linear Algebra for Engineering Practice 3

## Overview
This note summarizes the key components of Homework 3 for the MA1508E module, focusing on the concepts of subspaces, bases, and row spaces in linear algebra. The worksheet consists of three main questions, each addressing fundamental principles of vector spaces and matrix theory. Students are required to demonstrate their understanding through proofs and calculations, reinforcing their grasp of linear algebra concepts.

## Key Concepts & Definitions
- **Subspace**: A subset of a vector space that is closed under vector addition and scalar multiplication. [[Subspace]]
- **Basis**: A set of vectors in a vector space that are linearly independent and span the space. [[Basis]]
- **Dimension**: The number of vectors in a basis for a vector space, indicating the space's size. [[Dimension]]
- **Row Space**: The vector space generated by the rows of a matrix. [[Row Space]]
- **Reduced Row-Echelon Form (RREF)**: A form of a matrix that simplifies solving linear equations. [[RREF]]

## Detailed Technical Breakdown

### Question 1: Proving a Subspace
1. **Given Vector**: 
   \[
   V = \left\{ \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{pmatrix} : \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -2 & 1 \\ 0 & -4 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} y \\ z \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \right\}
   \]
2. **Proof Steps**:
   - Check closure under addition.
   - Check closure under scalar multiplication.
   - Find a basis for \( V \).
   - Determine the dimension of \( V \).

### Question 2: Basis Verification
1. **Set \( S \)**:
   \[
   S = \left\{ \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}, \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \\ -3 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix}, \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 0 \\ 4 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \right\}
   \]
   - **(a)** Check if \( S \) is a basis for \( V \).
   - **(b)** Let \( T = \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \). Show that \( T \) is a basis for \( V \).
   - **(c)** Let \( v = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ -2 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix} \). Show that \( v \) is in \( V \) and find coordinates relative to basis \( T \).

### Question 3: Matrix Analysis
1. **Matrix \( A \)**:
   \[
   A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 & -1 & 0 & 0 \\ 2 & 1 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & -2 & 0 & 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}
   \]
2. **(a)** Find the RREF of \( A \) and explain the derivation.
3. **(b)** Determine the nullspace of \( A \).
4. **(c)** Identify which vectors are in the row space of \( A \):
   - \( v_1 = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix} \)
   - \( v_2 = \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ -1 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix} \)

## Implementation/Examples
```plaintext
1. To prove \( V \) is a subspace:
   - Let \( u, v \in V \) and \( c \in \mathbb{R} \).
   - Show \( u + v \in V \) and \( cu \in V \).

2. For basis verification:
   - Use the determinant method or row reduction to check linear independence.

3. For RREF:
   - Apply Gaussian elimination to \( A \) to find its RREF.
```
