# Team-Based Workshop (TBW) Rubrics

## Overview
The Team-Based Workshop (TBW) Rubrics outline the expectations for active participation and effective collaboration within workshop sessions. These workshops are designed to foster co-creation and community learning, emphasizing the importance of engagement and teamwork. Understanding these rubrics is crucial for maximizing the learning experience and ensuring productive interactions among participants.

## Key Concepts & Definitions
- **Active Participation**: Engaging fully in discussions and activities, contributing meaningfully to team efforts.
- **Effective Collaboration**: Working respectfully and productively with teammates to achieve common goals.
- **Co-Creation**: The process of collaborating with others to generate new ideas or solutions.

## Detailed Technical Breakdown

### Active Participation
- **What it Looks Like**:
  - Being on time for classes
  - Staying consistently engaged
  - Contributing meaningfully to discussions
  - Completing assigned tasks with care and rigor
  - Taking initiative by connecting personal experiences to the learning material

- **What it Does Not Look Like**:
  - Limited engagement or focus during the session
  - Frequent distractions from devices (e.g., phones, laptops)
  - Being physically present but not contributing
  - Completing tasks with minimal effort or leaving parts unfinished
  - Inconsistent participation with noticeable drop-off

### Effective Collaboration
- **What it Looks Like**:
  - Collaborating respectfully and productively
  - Contributing thoughtfully to large group discussions

- **What it Does Not Look Like**:
  - Participating inconsistently
  - Engaging in minimal effort during group activities

## Implementation/Examples
```markdown
### Example of Active Participation
- A student shares a personal experience related to the workshop topic, enhancing the discussion.

### Example of Effective Collaboration
- Team members actively listen to each other, providing constructive feedback and building on each other's ideas.
```

> [!note] **Tip**: Always strive to connect your personal experiences to the workshop content to enhance engagement and learning.
> 
> [!important] **Reminder**: Effective collaboration requires respect and open communication among all team members to foster a positive learning environment.
