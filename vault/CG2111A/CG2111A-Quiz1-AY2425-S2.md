# CG2111A Engineering Principles and Practices II - Midterm Assessment Overview

## Overview
This document outlines the midterm assessment for CG2111A, consisting of fifteen questions focused on key concepts in engineering principles and practices. The assessment is designed to evaluate students' understanding of programming concepts, particularly in relation to the ATMega328P microcontroller and its functionalities. It is an open-book exam, allowing the use of printed notes, and emphasizes the importance of understanding both theoretical and practical aspects of the material.

## Key Concepts & Definitions
- **ATMega328P**: A microcontroller used in various embedded systems, known for its efficiency and versatility.
- **PWM (Pulse Width Modulation)**: A technique used to control the amount of power delivered to electrical devices by varying the width of the pulses in a pulse train.
- **Interrupts**: Mechanisms that allow a microcontroller to respond to events asynchronously, pausing the current execution to handle the event.
- **ADC (Analog to Digital Converter)**: A device that converts an analog signal into a digital signal.
- **CTC (Clear Timer on Compare Match)**: A mode of operation for timers in microcontrollers that resets the timer when it reaches a specified value.

## Detailed Technical Breakdown

### Assessment Instructions
- **Format**: 15 questions, including an appendix with register details.
- **Duration**: 60 minutes.
- **Marks**: Total of 30 marks.
- **Materials**: Open book; only printed notes allowed.
- **Prohibited Items**: No electronic devices except for calculators.

### Sample Questions Breakdown
1. **Code Analysis**:
   - Given code snippets involving bit manipulation and control of I/O pins.
   - Questions assess understanding of the code's functionality and expected outcomes.

2. **Timing and Frequency Calculations**:
   - Questions related to the timing of PWM signals and the implications of execution time on output accuracy.

3. **Interrupt Handling**:
   - Understanding the sequence of operations for handling interrupts and the implications of interrupt priorities.

4. **Data Transmission**:
   - Calculating transmission times and understanding the implications of data framing in serial communication.
