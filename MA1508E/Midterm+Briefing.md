# MA1508E Midterm Exam Briefing

## Overview
The MA1508E Midterm Exam is scheduled for 13 March 2026, from 1900 to 2000 hours, lasting 60 minutes. This open-book exam will be conducted face-to-face using Examplify, with specific guidelines on materials and devices allowed. Students must prepare by ensuring all necessary software is installed and must adhere to strict academic integrity standards.

## Key Concepts & Definitions
- **Examplify**: A software used for administering exams in a secure environment.
- **Open-book exam**: An exam format allowing students to refer to their notes and materials.
- **Academic integrity**: The ethical code governing academic conduct, prohibiting cheating and plagiarism.

## Detailed Technical Breakdown

### Exam Details
- **Date & Time**: 13 March 2026, 1900–2000hrs
- **Venue**: MPSH 1A, 1B
- **Format**: Face-to-face using [[Examplify]]
- **Coverage**: Sections 1.1 to 3.4
- **Structure**: 
  - 10 multiple response and fill-in-the-blank questions
  - All answers submitted via [[Examplify]]

### Preparation Before the Exam
- **Installations**:
  - Ensure [[Examplify]] is installed by 13 March 2026.
  - Install MATLAB (offline version) or a calculator app on the same device.
- **Venue Check**: Confirm exam venue and seat number on Canvas.
- **Device Requirements**:
  - Only laptops (Windows or Mac) are permitted; tablets and iPads are not allowed.

### On the Exam Day
- **Arrival**: Be at the venue by 1830hrs; entry starts at 1845hrs.
- **Identification**: Bring a physical student card or valid photo ID.
- **Device Preparation**: Ensure your laptop is charged and bring the charger.

### During the Exam
- **Procedure**:
  - Turn on your laptop and open notes before launching [[Examplify]].
  - A password will be provided to unlock the exam file.
- **Entry Rules**: Late entry is not allowed after 30 minutes; early exit is prohibited until the exam concludes.
- **Device Policy**: All mobile devices and smartwatches must be turned off and stored away.

### Question Format
- **Multiple Response Questions (MRQs)**:
  - No negative marking; only correct answers earn marks.
  - Selection limits apply based on the number of correct answers.
- **Fill-in-the-Blank Questions**:
  - Answers must be typed without extra spaces and in simplest form if a fraction.

> [!warning] **Academic Integrity Reminder**: All students must uphold the highest standards of academic integrity. The use of AI tools or LLMs is strictly prohibited during the exam.
